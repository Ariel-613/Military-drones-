    The Black Hornet

    The Black Hornet 4 represents the latest advancement in covert UAV technology, offering soldiers improved situational awareness and effectiveness with less mental strain. This drone features a high-resolution Thermal Imager (TI) and an Electro Optical (EO) camera with excellent low-light performance. It also boasts extended range and increased durability. With new obstacle avoidance abilities and an advanced battery, the Black Hornet 4 enhances reconnaissance capabilities, providing soldiers with crucial information for mission success.


    MQ 28

    The Boeing MQ-28 is set to deliver a game-changing advantage for both crewed and uncrewed missions conducted by allied forces. With its impressive 38-foot length and a range of over 2,000 nautical miles, it will offer performance akin to fighter aircraft. Additionally, it will feature integrated sensor packages to aid in tasks such as intelligence gathering, surveillance, reconnaissance, and tactical early warning missions. Furthermore, the MQ-28 will employ artificial intelligence for autonomous flight, either independently or in coordination with crewed aircraft, while ensuring a safe distance from other aircraft.


    MQ-9 Reaper

    The MQ-9 Reaper has dimensions of 36 feet in length and 12.5 feet in height, with a wingspan of 66 feet, and an empty weight of 4,900 pounds. It is powered by a Honeywell TPE331-10GD turboprop engine, capable of producing up to 900 shaft horsepower, resulting in a cruise speed of around 230 mph (equivalent to 200 knots). With a fuel capacity of 602 gallons, the drone can cover a range of 1,150 miles. It is capable of sustained flight at its maximum altitude of 50,000 feet for over 27 hours, conducting surveillance using advanced cameras, sensors, and radar. Moreover, the Reaper can carry a payload of up to 3,750 pounds, including various attack weapons.


    B.A.E. Systems Taranis

    Taranis is a significant unmanned combat aerial vehicle (UCAV) developed to showcase its capabilities in Intelligence, Surveillance, Target Acquisition, and Reconnaissance (ISTAR) missions. It excels in sustained surveillance, marking targets, gathering intelligence, deterring adversaries, and executing strikes in hostile regions. The UCAV possesses the ability to follow pre-set waypoints, manage takeoff and landing processes, and utilizes artificial intelligence features that enable it to adapt its operations to achieve specific objectives, using data collected during its flight.


    Bayraktar Kizilema

    The Bayraktar Kizilelma Fighter, developed by Baykar in Turkey, is an unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) known for its advanced maneuverability and stealthy design that makes it less susceptible to radar detection. Weighing 8.5 tons, including 1.5 tons of ordnance, and resembling a compact, pilotless fighter jet, it is powered by a Ukrainian Al-25T turbofan engine from Motor Sich. This UAV can achieve cruising speeds between 0.6 Mach and a maximum of 0.9 Mach, operating at altitudes of up to 30,000 feet with a combat radius of 500 nautical miles and a five-hour flight duration. It possesses a low radar cross-section (RCS), advanced situational awareness thanks to its Active Electronically Scanned Array (AESA) radar, and the capability for fully autonomous takeoff and landing. The Kizilema is equipped for both air-to-ground and air-to-air missions, and there are plans for the development of two supersonic variants using Ukrainian Ivchenko-Progress AI-322 afterburning engines.


    Bayraktar TB2

    Turkey's Baykar company has gained prominence as a leading international supplier of UAV and drone technology. Among its widely exported UAVs, the Bayraktar TB2 stands out for its impressive array of features compactly packed into a lightweight design. Notably, as it's developed in a NATO member nation, it adheres to at least some NATO operational standards, making it appealing to the 30 member states within the alliance. Recognizable by its distinctive inverted V-tail, the TB2 has garnered attention for its utilization by the Ukrainian Armed Forces during the 2022 Russo-Ukrainian conflict.


    AeroVironment Switchblade

    The Switchblade belongs to a unique category of UAVs that do not return after launch. It falls under the class of loitering munitions, a relatively recent and highly effective type of unmanned vehicle, often referred to by its manufacturer as a loitering missile. These drones are designed for targeting beyond visual range and are launched from lightweight, portable tubes that can be easily carried in a backpack and deployed within minutes. Once in flight, users can control the vehicle for up to ten minutes using a tablet-style controller with a camera feed from the drone. The Switchblade can either explode on impact or be repurposed for reconnaissance and safely recalled if a mission is aborted.
